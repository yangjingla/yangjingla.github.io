Graph Embedding

Why Embedding ?

Goal of Representation Learning is to automate the whole process by casting feature extraction as reprentation learning problem in which case we do not require any hand-engineered features.

  • Distribution Hypothesis which states that words in similar context tends to have similar meanings
  • Homophily Hypothesis : nodes that are highly interconnected and belong to similar network clusters or commutities should be embedded closely.
  • Structure equivalence hypothesis : nodes that have similar structural roles in networks should be embedded closely together

Many important tasks in network analysis involve prediction of nodes and edges.Such as ,Linking Predictions , Node Classification .

DeepWalk

Problem

A novel approach for learning latent representation of a vertices in a network, encoding social relations in a continuous vector space (discrete feature -> continuous feature ),which is eaily easily exploited by stastistical models.

Learning Social representations

  • Adaptability , Community aware , Low diemensional , Continuous

Motivation

Word2vec and Language model : Learning representation for vertices from a stream of short random walks using optimization techniques originally designed for language modeling.

power-laws connections: degree distribution of a connected graph follow power-law distributions.Surprisely ， the frequency which vertices appear in the short random walk will also follow a power-law distribution .

  • Hierarchical SoftMax: using huffman tree to optimization $O(V) - > O(logV)$

Node2vec:Scalable Feature Learning for Networks

Problem

we learn a mapping of nodes to low-demensions space of feature that maximizes the likelihood of preserving network neighborhoods of nodes.

learning feature of representation by sloving an optmization problem , which involves a trade-off computational complexity and accuracy.e

Motivation

flexible notions nodes features (use structural equivalence information )

  • Structural Equivalence Hypothesis: nodes in network could be organized based on commutities they belong to..

    rigid notions of nodes neighborhoods(DeepWalk)

  • largely insentive to connectivity patterns unique to networks

  • Fail to offer flexiablility in sampling of nodes from a network.[word2vec’s skip-gram model can be sampling problem]

In Figure 1, we observe nodes u and $s_1$ belonging to the same tightly knit community of nodes, while the nodes u and $s_6$ in the two distinct communities share the same structural role of a hub node. Real-world networks commonly exhibit a mixture of such equivalences.

Methtod

Optimize Objection

  • Step1:We formulated featuring learning in network as a maximum likelihood optimization problem.(VS The word feature representations are learned by optmizing the likelihood objective using SGD with negative sampling)

$$ max_{f} \sum_{u \in V} log Pr(N_s(u) | f(u))$$

Contribution: attention that $N_s(u)$ are not stricted to just immediate neighbors but vastly different structures depending on the sampling strategy [structual commutity feature as figure 1].

  • Step 2:two assumption to simiplied equation
    • conditional assumption

$$ Pr(N_s(u) | f(u)) = \prod_{n_i \in N_s(u) Pr(n_i | f(u))}$$

  • Symmetry in feature space(for modeling pairs of node , just define dot-product of pair nodes)

$$Pr(n_i | f(u)) = \frac{exp(f(n_i) \cdot f(u))}{\sum_{v \in V} exp(f(v) \cdot f(u))}$$

  • Step3: Finally simplifies as fllow:

$$max_f \sum_{v \in V} [-log (\sum_{v \in V} exp(f(u)\cdot f(n_i)))] + \sum_{n_i \in N_s(u)} f(n_i) \cdot f(n_i)$$

Sampling strategy

  • BFS & DFS
    • BFS can explore only limited neighborhoods , which makes BFS suitable for characterizing structural equivalence in network which rely on ther immediate local structural of network with low Variance.
    • DFS can freely explore network neighboorhoods which is important in discovering homophiles commuatities at the cost of high variance.
  • *Biased 2^nd RandomWalk (with Search bias $\alpha$) *
    • two parameters (return parameters p and In-out parameter q Figure2)
    • computationally efficient in terms of both time and space complexity ,which time complexity $O(\frac{l}{k(l-k)})$ , space complexity $O(\alpha^{2} |V|)$

Future & Conclusion

  • ✅Structual Equivalenve Hypothesis is helpful to learning feature by representation. Node2vec is expressive enough to capture the diversity of conncectivity patterns observed in network.
