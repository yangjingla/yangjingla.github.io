Problem

  • Character-based methods ,however ,is that explicited word and sequence information is not fully exploited.

Motivation

  • ecnodes a sequence of input characters as well as all potential words that match a lexicon.
  • Pipeline: segmentation -> NER
  • Design a novel lattice LSTM representation for mixed characters and lexicon words , use a word-character lattice for segemention-free chinese NER

Method

  • Lattice structured RNNS can be viewed as a natural extension of tree-structed RNNS to DAG.

  • Word-Based Modle

    • Word: $w^{w}i = e^w(w_i)$ , word+ charlstm : $ X_i^{w} = [e^w (w_i) ; x_i^c]$$,$ $X_i^{c} = [h^c{t(i , len(i))} ; h^c_{t(i ,1)}]$ , word+charCNN: $x_i^c = max_{t(i,1) \le j \le t(i,len(i))} (W^T_{CNN} \begin{bmatrix} e^C(c_{j - \frac{ke-1}{2}})\ \cdots \ e^c(c_{j- \frac{ke-1}{2}})\end{bmatrix} + b_{CNN})$

TENER: adapting transformer encoder for named entity recongnition[acl2019]

Problem

  • Transformer boardly adopted in various NLP tasks owing to its parallelism and advantage in modeing long context, but vanilla Transfomer in NER is not good.
  • position embedding used in villina transformer is aware the distance but unaware of the directionality. it is not easy for transformer to distinguish which side the context information comes from.
  • CNN is not perfect for representing character-level information, but transformer-based character-level encoder.

Motivation

  • RELATIVE POSITION ENCODING : Both direction and distance is important in NER tasks.
  • SHARP ATTENTION: sparse attention is more suitable since not all words are necessary to be attended in ner.
  • CHARACTER-LEVEL: extract both word-level and character-level information in a word.

Method

  • Direction and Distance-Aware Attention

    • Sinusoidal position embedding lacks directionality

  • Sharper attetion

    • drop scaling factor $\sqrt{d_k}$ in attention(Q,K,V)

  • CRF Layer [review]

    • Input $s = [s_1 , s_2 , \cdots,s_T]$ , $y = [y_1 , y_2 , \cdots , y_T]$

    $$ P(y|s) = \frac{\sum^{T}{t=1} e^{f(y{t-1} , y_t , s)}}{ \sum^{Y(s)}{y^{\prime}} \sum^{T}{t=1} e^{f(y^{\prime}_{t-1}, y^{\prime}_t , s )}}$$

    • transition score : $f(y_{t-1} , y_t , s)$ , Viterbi algorithm to max $P(y|s)$

  • Experiments

Conlusion & Qusetion

  • directional aware positional encoding ; un-scaled sharper attention

LR-CNN: CNN-Based Chinese NER with Lexicon Rethinking [ijcai19]

Problem

  • CNN->RNN-based methods fail to fully exploit GPU parallelism because of the recurrent structure, which limits their computational efficience.
  • Rethinking Mechanism ->Have difficulty dealing with conflicts between potential words being incorporated in the lexicon: one character may correspond to couples of potential words in the lexicon, and this conflict can misguide the model such that it predicts different labels.

Motivation

  • CNN to process the whole sentence as well as all the potential words in parallel. The intuition is that when the window size of the convolution operation is set to 2, then all the potential words can easily fuse into corresponding positions[KIM 2004]

  • They have no chance to modify conflicting lexicon information after seeing the whole sentence. By adding a feedback layer and feeding back the high-level features [Ma et al., 2014], this rethinking mechanism can leverage the high-level semantic to refine the weights of embedded words and address the conflicts between potential words. Experimental.[Ma et al., 2014]

Conclusion

  • Previous attempts to use a rethinking mechanism in neural networks have been made in image classification to tackle issues of occlusion and noise
  • Lattice LSTM suffers from inefficiency and word conflict. Our approach extends these ideas from the parallel modeling of characters with potential words and a rethinking mechanism. Hence, our method is more efficient and effective.

FLAT: Chinese NER Using Flat-Lattice Transformer [acl20]

Problem

  • Due to the dynamic lattice structure, these methods cannot fully utilize the parallel computation of GPU.

  • two lines of methods to leverage the. lattice.

    • One line is to design a model to be compatible with lattice input, such as lattice LSTM (Zhang and Yang, 2018) and LR-CNN (Gui et al., 2019a)
    • Another line is to convert lattice into graph and use a graph neural network (GNN) to encode it, such as Lexicon-based Graph Network (LGN) (Gui et al., 2019b) and Collaborative Graph Network (CGN) (Sui et al., 2019). While

Motivation

  • The lattice structure has been proved to have a great benefit to utilize the word information and avoid the error propagation of word segmentation.
  • Transformer (Vaswani et al., 2017) adopts fully-connected self- attention to model the long-distance dependencies in a sequence.
  • How to use transformer to model lattice structures?
    • self-attention mechanism brings two ad vantages over lattice LSTM: 1) All characters can directly interact with its self-matched words. 2) Long-distance dependencies can be fully modeled.

Method

  • Relative Position Encoding of Spans

    • For two spans $x_i $and $x_j$ in the lattice, there are three kinds of relations between them: intersec- tion, inclusion and separation, determined by their heads and tails.

  • 🍅position encoding in transformer :

    ​ $$ \begin{aligned} p_d^{2k} &= sin(\frac{d}{ 10000^{\frac{2k}{d_{model}}}} ) \ p_d^{2k+1} &= cos(\frac{d}{10000^{\frac{2k}{d_{model}}}})\end{aligned}$$

    where d is $d(hh){ij} , d(ht){ij} , d(th){ij} , d(tt){ij} $and k denotes the index of dimension of position encoding

Conclusion& Qustion

  • Compared with TENER, FLAT leverages lexicon resources and uses a new position encoding.
  • NER’s strong alignment of label and input,we use a well-designed position encoding to indicate the lattice structure。
