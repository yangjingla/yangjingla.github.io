LATTICE-LSTM[ACL2018]

Problem

  • chinese NER is correlated with word segmentation errors ,how combine character-level and word-level informations?
  • OOV problem
  • Character-based methods ,however ,is that explicited word and sequence information is not fully exploited.

Motivation

  • ecnodes a sequence of input characters as well as all potential words that match a lexicon.
  • Pipeline: segmentation -> NER
  • Design a novel lattice LSTM representation for mixed characters and lexicon words , use a word-character lattice for segemention-free chinese NER

Method

  • Lattice structured RNNS can be viewed as a natural extension of tree-structed RNNS to DAG.

Two are Better than One:Joint Entity and Relation Extraction with Table-Sequence Encoders [acl2020]

Problem

  • pipeline method vs jointly method ?
  • jointly method as table filling problem?
    • First, these methods typically suffer from feature confusion as they use a single representation for the two tasks – NER and RE.
    • Second, these methods underutilize the table structure as they usually convert it to a sequence and then use a sequence labeling approach to fill the table

Motivation

NER is then regarded as a sequence labeling problem where tags are assigned along the diagonal entries of the table. RE is regarded as the problem of labeling other entries within the table

  • sequence representations and table repre-sentations for NER and RE respectively
  • we design a mechanism to allow them to interact with each other, in order to take advantage of the inherent association underlying the NER and RE tasks。

  • Use the attention weights of BERT for learning table representations.

Method

  • table encoder for table representation

    • an N × N table of vectors, where the vector at row i and column j corresponds to the i-th and j-th word of the input sentence.
    • MD-RNN: multi-dimensional adaptations of GRU

  • sequence encoder for sequence representation

    • table-guide attention

    ​ $$f(Query_j ,Key_j ) = U \cdot g(Query_j , Key_j) \ T_{l ,i,j} = g(S_{l-1 ,i} , S_{l-1,j}) = g(Query_j , Key_j)$$

  • Training and Evaluation

    • $$SKIP😮‍💨$$

Conclusion

  • Pipeline Vs Joint : Joint learning has been shown effective since it can alleviate the error propagation issue and benefit from exploiting the interrelation between NER and RE。
  • Metric: Following the established line of work, we use the F1 measure to evaluate the performance ofNER and RE. For NER, an entity prediction is correct if and only if its type and boundaries both match with those of a gold entity. For RE, a relation prediction is considered correct if its relation type and the boundaries of the two entities match with those in the gold data. We also report the strict relation F1 (denoted RE+), where a relation prediction is considered correct if its relation type as well as the boundaries and types of the two entities all match with those in the gold data. Relations are asymmetric, so the order of the two entities in a relation matters.
  • attention visualize : We visualize the table-guided attention with bertviz https://github.com/jessevig/bertviz
  • Future : In the future, we would like to inves tigate how the table representation may be applied to other tasks. Another direction is to generalize the way in which the table and sequence interact to other.
